Code FizzBuzz

Write a program that prints out the numbers 1 to 100 (inclusive).
If the number is divisible by 3, print Fizz instead of the number. If it's divisible by 5, print Buzz. If it's divisible by both 3 and 5, print FizzBuzz. 

start = 1
finish = 100

#all numbers in given range divisible by 3
threes = []
for i in range(start, finish / 3 + 1):
    threes.append(i * 3);

#all numbers in given range divisible by 5
fives = []
for i in range(start, finish / 5 + 1):
    fives.append(i * 5);

#print it out
for i in range(start, finish + 1):
    if (i in threes) and (i in fives):
            print "FizzBuzz"
    elif i in threes:
            print "Fizz"
    elif i in fives:
            print "Buzz"
    else:   
            print i

What is the most fascinating thing you've learned in the past month?

I've learned that some big astronomers classify Earth and Moon as dual planetary system. The tides are possible because of Moon's ability to attract the water. The interesting thing is, Moon pulls on water slowing down the Earth, and water in turn pulls on Moon making it spin faster. In a million or billion years Moon and Earth will synchronize, so the Moon will be making a full spin in 24 hours thus always being in one spot on the skies.

Explain something you understand deeply as clearly as you can.

I like simple designs. Take for example, a thermostat of an engine cooling system.

When you start the engine, you want to reach working temperature before hitting high RPM's. Thus you need coolant to not move in the cooling system. Once it's at working temperature, you want to keep temps at constant level so the thermostat opens and lets coolant run. If temperature drops, thermostat closes again.

I used this example to explain boolean logic to my students.
